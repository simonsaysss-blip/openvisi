# The AI Visibility Era Needs a New Measurement Language

Search is shifting from links to answers.

Brands need a new measurement language.

OpenVisi defines an open-source vocabulary and measurement layer for AI
Visibility.

## Why Traditional SEO Language Is Insufficient

Traditional search language was built around pages, links, rankings, snippets,
and traffic attribution. AI-generated answers introduce a different surface:
systems synthesize answers, compare alternatives, cite unevenly, and may route
attention without showing a familiar ranked list.

That does not make existing search work irrelevant. It means teams need a
separate measurement language for answer presence, entity accuracy, citation
quality, and competitive displacement.

## Why Vocabulary Matters

Teams cannot improve what they cannot name consistently. If every report uses a
different term for the same signal, benchmarks become hard to compare and
methodology becomes hard to audit.

OpenVisi uses canonical terms so reports, schemas, docs, benchmarks, and future
CLI output describe the same measurement surface.

## Why Open-source Methodology Matters

AI Visibility should be explainable. Open methodology allows builders,
maintainers, researchers, and operators to inspect definitions, challenge
assumptions, reproduce scans, and improve the model in public.

OpenVisi should not hide key decisions behind black-box scoring.

## Four Core Dimensions

Presence: Does the AI-generated answer mention the entity?

Accuracy: Does the answer describe the entity correctly?

Citation: Which sources does the answer use to understand or support the entity?

Competition: Does the answer route the user toward competitors?

## Why OpenVisi Is Building in Public

The category is early. The vocabulary, prompt packs, scoring, and benchmark
format should be discussed in the open before they harden into defaults.

OpenVisi is building in public to make AI Visibility measurable, explainable,
and reproducible without unsupported claims about proprietary AI systems.
