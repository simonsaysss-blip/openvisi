# AI Citation Signals Are Not Backlinks, But They Matter

AI Citation Signals are public signals that increase the likelihood that an
entity or source can be cited in AI-generated answers.

They are not backlinks.

Backlinks are part of the web's link graph. AI Citation Signals are broader.
They include official source clarity, structured data, freshness, documentation
depth, third-party references, and stable URLs that can support answer
generation.

## Citation Coverage

Citation Coverage measures how often AI-generated answers cite or rely on
sources connected to the target entity.

An entity can be mentioned without being cited. That distinction matters because
citations can reveal which public sources the answer surface uses to support its
claims.

## Machine-readable Trust

Machine-readable Trust is about signals that help machines assess credibility,
authority, and freshness.

Examples include structured data, author or organization clarity, source
freshness, documentation depth, third-party citations, and consistency across
official sources.

## Why This Matters

LLM-powered discovery needs trust layers that machines can parse. AI Citation
Signals may become one of those layers, especially for entities that need their
facts, category, and authority to be represented accurately.

The right goal is not to manufacture signals. The goal is to make real evidence
clear, stable, and citeable.

OpenVisi measures these signals so teams can see where their public source layer
is strong, weak, or missing.
