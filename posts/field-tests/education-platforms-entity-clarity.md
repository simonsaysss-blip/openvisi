# Education Platforms Need Course and Entity Clarity

OpenVisi ran a small crawl-diagnostic scan across Coursera, Khan Academy, and
Duolingo on May 26, 2026.

Education is a useful category for AI Visibility because AI-generated answers
often summarize who a platform is for, what it teaches, and which learning path
fits a user.

## The Signal

| Domain          | AI Visibility Score | Entity Clarity | Citation Coverage Proxy |
| --------------- | ------------------- | -------------- | ----------------------- |
| coursera.org    | 71                  | 0.95           | 0.75                    |
| duolingo.com    | 42                  | 0.37           | 0.30                    |
| khanacademy.org | 32                  | 0.25           | 0.30                    |

The results varied widely. Some public homepages exposed clear category and
course-like language. Others relied more on brand familiarity or app-like flows
that were thinner for the lightweight crawler.

## Why This Matters

Education platforms need AI systems to understand:

- what the entity is
- what subjects or courses it covers
- who it serves
- what level or learning path it supports
- how it differs from alternatives

That is Entity Clarity.

## Follow-up

Homepage scans are not enough for this category. Course pages, subject hubs,
help centers, and institutional pages should be tested separately.
