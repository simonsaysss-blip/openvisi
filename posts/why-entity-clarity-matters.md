# Why Entity Clarity Matters in LLM Search

Entity Clarity measures whether AI systems understand what a company, product,
or project is, what category it belongs to, who it serves, and how it differs
from alternatives.

This sounds simple. In practice, it is one of the most common sources of bad AI
answers.

## The Failure Modes

An entity can be described too vaguely. It can be placed in the wrong category.
It can be represented with outdated positioning. It can be confused with a
competitor that has clearer public documentation.

These are not only copywriting problems. They are measurement problems.

## AI-readable Structure

Entity Clarity improves when public information is organized in an AI-readable
Structure.

That includes stable product pages, clear docs, structured FAQs, comparison
pages, schema metadata, and direct category language.

AI systems need source material they can parse, classify, and cite.

## Narrative Accuracy

Narrative Accuracy asks whether the generated answer describes the entity
correctly.

If the source material is inconsistent, thin, or outdated, the answer may still
mention the entity while explaining it poorly.

## How Teams Can Improve

Start with a canonical entity definition. State the category. Name the audience.
Describe the product or service in concrete terms. Explain how it differs from
alternatives.

Then make that language consistent across the homepage, docs, metadata,
comparison pages, and reports.

OpenVisi treats Entity Clarity as a core metric because AI Visibility depends on
being understood before being recommended or cited.
