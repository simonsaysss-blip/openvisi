# AI-readable Structure

One-line definition: AI-readable Structure is the way content is organized so AI
systems can parse, classify, and cite it.

## Why It Matters

AI systems need stable, explicit, well-structured source material before they can
summarize an entity accurately or cite it reliably.

## How OpenVisi Measures It

OpenVisi looks for crawlable pages, headings, definitions, FAQ patterns, stable
URLs, schema metadata, and content that separates product facts from general
copy.

Examples:

- clear docs
- structured FAQ
- comparison pages
- stable URLs
- schema metadata
- clear product/category pages

## How Teams Can Improve It

Use canonical URLs, add structured documentation, keep product and category
pages distinct, publish answer-ready FAQs, and use schema metadata where it
matches real page content.

## Related OpenVisi Metrics

`promptCoverage`, `entityClarity`, `citationCoverage`,
`machineReadableTrust`.
